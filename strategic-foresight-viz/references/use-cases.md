## How to Use This Guide

If you're not sure which foresight technique to use, find your situation below. This guide maps common strategic questions and scenarios to the visualization techniques that will help you answer them. Choose either by **foresight phase** (the stage of your analysis) or by **question type** (the specific question you're trying to answer).

---

## Selection by Foresight Phase

### Phase 1: Environmental Scanning ("What's happening around us?")

**When to use:** Starting a new foresight exercise, need to understand the landscape

**Techniques:** PESTLE/GRNO Scan, Macro/Meso/Micro Analysis

**Typical scenario:** "We need to map the political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental factors affecting our organization at local through global levels."

**Example prompts:**
- "Create a PESTLE scan for cybersecurity threats in the EU financial sector"
- "Map the macro/meso/micro factors affecting digital transformation in government"

---

### Phase 2: Trend Identification ("What patterns are emerging?")

**When to use:** After scanning, need to identify and prioritize trends

**Techniques:** Trend Stage Analysis, Trend Impact Analysis (Layered + Temporal), Trend-on-Trend Cross-Impact, Countertrends & Cycles

**Typical scenario:** "We've identified several trends and need to understand their maturity, impact, and interactions."

**Example prompts:**
- "Plot our 5 key trends on a stage-impact matrix"
- "Show how these trends interact with each other over 1-10 years"
- "Which trends are cyclical vs. structural?"

---

### Phase 3: Early Warning ("What signals should we watch for?")

**When to use:** Need to detect emerging issues before they become trends

**Techniques:** Perception Analysis (See/Hear/Think/Feel), What/So What/Now What, Weak Signal Analysis I & II

**Typical scenario:** "We want to identify early indicators of change that haven't become mainstream trends yet."

**Example prompts:**
- "Map our team's perceptions about AI regulation"
- "Analyze weak signals around quantum computing threats"
- "What are we missing in our current monitoring?"

---

### Phase 4: Scenario Development ("What futures might unfold?")

**When to use:** Need to explore alternative futures for strategic planning

**Techniques:** Three Futures, Futures Cone, Dator's Four Archetypes, Intuitive Logics, Lum's Four Steps

**Typical scenario:** "We need to develop plausible future scenarios to stress-test our strategy."

**Example prompts:**
- "Create best/likely/worst case scenarios for post-quantum cryptography readiness"
- "Map our futures using Dator's archetypes for the energy transition"
- "What's possible, plausible, and probable for our market in 5 years?"

---

### Phase 5: Impact & Risk Assessment ("What could go wrong? What should we prepare for?")

**When to use:** Testing scenarios against organizational readiness

**Techniques:** VUCA Analysis, CATUR Analysis, What If/Then Cross-Impact, Consequence Analysis, Threat/Opportunity Analysis

**Typical scenario:** "We need to assess the volatility, uncertainty, complexity, and ambiguity of our operating environment."

**Example prompts:**
- "Create a VUCA assessment of supply chain disruptions"
- "Map what-if scenarios for a major data breach"
- "Which risks have cascading consequences across departments?"

---

### Phase 6: Historical & Comparative Analysis ("What can history teach us?")

**When to use:** Drawing lessons from historical parallels

**Techniques:** Analogical Reasoning, Layered Timeline

**Typical scenario:** "We want to compare our current situation with a historical precedent to draw strategic lessons."

**Example prompts:**
- "Compare the current AI governance challenge with the early internet regulation era"
- "Create a layered timeline showing daily life, systems, and values changes"
- "What happened the last time our industry faced similar disruption?"

---

### Phase 7: Stakeholder & Strategy ("Who are the actors and what's the strategy?")

**When to use:** Mapping stakeholders and strategic responses across scenarios

**Techniques:** Stakeholder Analysis (Lum), Intuitive Logics (strategic planning table)

**Typical scenario:** "We need to understand who will resist, channel, or accelerate change under different disruption levels."

**Example prompts:**
- "Map key stakeholders by power and interest in quantum computing transition"
- "Who wins and loses under each scenario? What's their strategy?"
- "What coalitions might form?"

## Common Foresight Workflows

### Full Foresight Cycle

1. **PESTLE/GRNO Scan** — Map the external environment
2. **Trend Stage Analysis** — Assess trend maturity
3. **Trend Impact Analysis** — Understand impact trajectories
4. **Weak Signals** — Identify early warning indicators
5. **Three Futures or Dator's Archetypes** — Develop scenarios
6. **VUCA/CATUR Assessment** — Evaluate environment complexity
7. **What If/Then** — Stress-test strategy
8. **Stakeholder Analysis** — Plan engagement and response

**Use for:** Strategic planning exercises, annual foresight cycles, comprehensive market assessments

---

### Quick Strategic Scan

1. **PESTLE Scan** — Fast environmental overview
2. **Three Futures** — Quick scenario thinking
3. **VUCA Assessment** — Risk environment snapshot

**Use for:** Executive briefings, rapid decision-making, board presentations

---

### Deep Scenario Planning

1. **Trend Analysis suite** (Stage + Impact + Cross-Impact) — Understand trend foundation
2. **Intuitive Logics** — Build detailed scenarios
3. **Consequence Analysis** — Explore implications
4. **Stakeholder Analysis** — Plan responses

**Use for:** Long-range strategic planning, major investment decisions, organizational transformation

---

### Emerging Threat Assessment

1. **Weak Signals** — Detect early warning signs
2. **What If/Then** — Model threat progression
3. **CATUR Analysis** — Assess complexity and risk
4. **Consequence Analysis** — Understand cascading effects

**Use for:** Crisis preparation, risk management, competitive intelligence

---

### Historical Lessons Applied

1. **Analogical Reasoning** — Find historical parallel
2. **Layered Timeline** — Compare periods side-by-side
3. **Three Futures** — Apply lessons to future scenarios

**Use for:** Learning from precedents, informed risk assessment, cultural change initiatives

## Tips for Success

- **Start with your question, not the technique.** What do you need to decide or understand? Work backward to the right visualization.
- **Combine techniques.** Most real foresight work uses multiple techniques in sequence. The workflows above show natural progressions.
- **Iterate and refine.** Foresight is not one-time analysis. Use these tools to deepen understanding through multiple passes.
- **Involve your team.** The best insights come when diverse stakeholders contribute their perspectives to scanning, trend assessment, and scenario building.
- **Keep it action-oriented.** The goal isn't beautiful visualizations—it's better strategic decisions. Always ask: "How does this change what we do?"
